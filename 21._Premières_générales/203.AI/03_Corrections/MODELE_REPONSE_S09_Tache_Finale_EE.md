# MODÈLE DE RÉPONSE - Tâche Finale S09
## "Should students use AI tools at school?"

**Séance 9 - Expression Écrite (EE) - Évaluation finale /20**

---

## CONSIGNE COMPLÈTE

**After our class debate, write your personal opinion: Should students use AI tools at school?**

**Attentes:**
- Texte argumentatif structuré
- Longueur: 100-120 mots
- Position claire (pour ou contre)
- 2-3 arguments avec exemples
- Contre-argument possible
- Vocabulaire de la séquence
- Structures grammaticales correctes (modaux, connecteurs)

## CRITÈRES DÉTAILLÉS

### 1. Position claire /4 pts

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **4 pts** | Position très claire dès l'introduction, reformulée en conclusion |
| **3 pts** | Position claire mais pas assez affirmée |
| **2 pts** | Position présente mais vague ou ambiguë |
| **1 pt** | Position peu claire ou contradictoire |
| **0 pt** | Pas de position identifiable |

**Exemples de bonnes formulations:**
- "I strongly believe that..."
- "In my opinion, students should/shouldn't..."
- "I am convinced that..."
- "From my perspective..."

---

### 2. Arguments et exemples /6 pts

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **6 pts** | 3 arguments développés avec exemples concrets et pertinents |
| **5 pts** | 2-3 arguments clairs avec exemples, bien développés |
| **4 pts** | 2 arguments corrects mais peu développés |
| **3 pts** | 2 arguments vagues ou sans exemples |
| **2 pts** | 1 seul argument développé |
| **1 pt** | Arguments très vagues ou hors sujet |
| **0 pt** | Pas d'arguments |

**Structure attendue pour chaque argument:**
- Idée principale (topic sentence)
- Explication ou développement
- Exemple concret (optionnel mais valorisé)

---

### 3. Contre-argument /3 pts

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **3 pts** | Contre-argument clair + réfutation ou nuance |
| **2 pts** | Contre-argument mentionné mais pas développé |
| **1 pt** | Allusion à une opinion contraire |
| **0 pt** | Pas de contre-argument |

**Formulations attendues:**
- "However, some people argue that..."
- "On the other hand..."
- "Although... , I still believe..."
- "Critics say that... , but..."

---

### 4. Vocabulaire /4 pts

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **4 pts** | Utilisation riche et appropriée du vocabulaire de la séquence (8+ mots) |
| **3 pts** | Bon usage du vocabulaire (5-7 mots de la séquence) |
| **2 pts** | Vocabulaire basique mais correct (3-4 mots) |
| **1 pt** | Vocabulaire pauvre ou répétitif (1-2 mots) |
| **0 pt** | Aucun vocabulaire de la séquence |

**Vocabulaire attendu (exemples):**
- AI tools, chatbot, narrow/general AI
- foundational skills, literacy
- to ban, forbidden fruit
- career preparation
- critical thinking
- to solve problems, to carry out tasks
- responsible/ethical use

---

### 5. Grammaire et structures /3 pts

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **3 pts** | Excellent : structures variées, modaux, connecteurs, peu d'erreurs |
| **2 pts** | Bien : phrases correctes, quelques erreurs mineures |
| **1 pt** | Passable : erreurs fréquentes mais texte compréhensible |
| **0 pt** | Insuffisant : erreurs majeures compromettant la compréhension |

**Structures attendues:**
- Modaux: should, must, can, might, could
- Connecteurs: however, moreover, furthermore, although, because
- Phrases complexes: when, if, which, that

**Pénalités:**
- -0.25 pt par erreur de conjugaison
- -0.25 pt par erreur de structure
- Maximum -3 pts pour la grammaire

## EXEMPLE 1 : TEXTE EXCELLENT (19/20)

### Position: FOR (POUR l'utilisation de l'IA)

*I strongly believe that students should use AI tools at school because they can enhance learning when used responsibly. First, AI provides immediate help, like having a private tutor available 24/7, which is especially crucial for students who need extra support. Moreover, AI literacy is a foundational skill for future careers, so banning these tools would be counterproductive. However, some teachers argue that AI prevents students from developing critical thinking skills. While this concern is valid, I believe that with proper guidance, students can use AI to solve problems more efficiently without losing their ability to think independently. Schools must teach students how to carry out tasks with AI ethically rather than forbidding it completely.* (119 mots)

**Évaluation:**
- Position claire: 4/4
- Arguments et exemples: 6/6 (3 arguments bien développés)
- Contre-argument: 3/3 (mentionné et réfuté)
- Vocabulaire: 4/4 (13 mots de la séquence: AI tools, responsibly, private tutor, 24/7, crucial, AI literacy, foundational skill, careers, banning, critical thinking skills, solve problems, carry out tasks, ethically)
- Grammaire: 2/3 (excellente mais une structure pourrait être plus variée)
- **TOTAL: 19/20**

## EXEMPLE 2 : TEXTE TRÈS BON (16/20)

### Position: AGAINST (CONTRE l'utilisation de l'IA)

*In my opinion, students shouldn't use AI at school during their foundational years. Students need to develop basic skills like writing, critical thinking and problem-solving before they can use AI effectively. When students rely on chatbots to complete homework, they don't learn anything – they just copy answers. Furthermore, using AI is like forbidden fruit: if schools ban it, students will want to use it even more, but this doesn't mean we should allow it. Some people say AI prepares students for their future careers. However, I believe that foundational skills are more important than learning AI tools, which they can learn later on the job.* (111 mots)

**Évaluation:**
- Position claire: 4/4
- Arguments et exemples: 5/6 (3 arguments mais peu d'exemples concrets)
- Contre-argument: 2/3 (mentionné mais peu développé)
- Vocabulaire: 3/4 (9 mots: AI, foundational, skills, critical thinking, problem-solving, chatbots, forbidden fruit, careers, foundational skills)
- Grammaire: 2/3 (quelques erreurs mineures de ponctuation)
- **TOTAL: 16/20**

## EXEMPLE 3 : TEXTE BON (14/20)

### Position: FOR (POUR avec nuances)

*I think that students should use AI tools at school but with strict rules. AI can help students understand difficult topics and solve problems faster. For example, in math class, AI can explain step-by-step solutions. However, teachers must make sure that students don't just copy answers. They should develop their critical thinking first. Some educators believe AI is dangerous because students become lazy, but I disagree. If we teach AI literacy correctly, students can learn foundational skills and use technology at the same time. Schools should not ban AI completely because it's part of our future.* (105 mots)

**Évaluation:**
- Position claire: 3/4 (position claire mais "I think" est moins fort)
- Arguments et exemples: 4/6 (2 arguments avec un bon exemple en maths)
- Contre-argument: 2/3 (mentionné brièvement)
- Vocabulaire: 3/4 (8 mots: AI tools, solve problems, critical thinking, AI literacy, foundational skills, ban, educators, dangerous)
- Grammaire: 2/3 (correcte mais structures simples)
- **TOTAL: 14/20**

## PHRASES UTILES POUR LES ÉLÈVES

### Introduction (Position)
- ✓ I strongly believe that...
- ✓ In my opinion, students should/shouldn't...
- ✓ I am convinced that...
- ✓ From my perspective...

### Arguments
- ✓ First / Firstly / To begin with...
- ✓ Moreover / Furthermore / In addition...
- ✓ Another reason is that...
- ✓ For example / For instance...

### Contre-argument
- ✓ However, some people argue that...
- ✓ On the other hand...
- ✓ Although critics say that... , I believe...
- ✓ While it's true that... , I still think...

### Conclusion
- ✓ In conclusion / To sum up...
- ✓ Therefore / Thus...
- ✓ For these reasons, I believe...

## ERREURS FRÉQUENTES À ÉVITER

### Contenu
- ❌ Pas de position claire
- ❌ Arguments trop vagues ("AI is good/bad")
- ❌ Pas d'exemples concrets
- ❌ Oubli du contre-argument
- ❌ Texte trop court (< 90 mots) ou trop long (> 140 mots)

### Vocabulaire
- ❌ Répétition excessive du mot "AI" (varier: AI tools, these technologies, etc.)
- ❌ Pas d'utilisation du vocabulaire de la séquence
- ❌ Vocabulaire trop basique (good, bad, nice, etc.)

### Grammaire
- ❌ Absence de modaux (should, must, can, etc.)
- ❌ Pas de connecteurs (but, however, moreover, etc.)
- ❌ Phrases trop simples (Subject + Verb + Object répétitif)
- ❌ Erreurs de temps (présent pour parler du futur)
